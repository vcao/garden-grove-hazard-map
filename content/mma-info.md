## What is methyl methacrylate?

Methyl methacrylate (MMA) is a colorless, flammable liquid with a sharp, fruity odor. It is the building block for poly(methyl methacrylate) (PMMA / Plexiglas / acrylic) and is widely used in the production of acrylic sheet, coatings, adhesives, and certain medical and dental materials.

## Why is the leak dangerous?

- **Highly flammable.** Flash point ~10 °C (50 °F). Vapors readily form ignitable mixtures with air.
- **Vapor-cloud explosion risk.** Released vapors can drift toward ignition sources and detonate.
- **Inhalation hazard.** Causes eye, nose, and throat irritation at low concentrations; higher exposures can trigger headaches, dizziness, and respiratory irritation.
- **Skin/eye contact** can cause irritation and possible sensitization with repeated exposure.

## Symptoms of exposure

- Sharp, fruity odor (the smell threshold is well below the hazard threshold)
- Watery, stinging eyes
- Coughing, sore throat, shortness of breath
- Headache, dizziness, nausea

If you experience any of these and you are downwind of the incident site, leave the area immediately and seek fresh air. Call **911** for emergencies.
